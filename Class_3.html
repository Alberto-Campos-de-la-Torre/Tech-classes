Topic Development: A Brand's DNA

What is a Brand? It's a Feeling.

A brand is much more than a logo or a name. It's the overall feeling, reputation, and experience people have when they think of your cause. A strong brand builds trust and makes your message memorable. For a social campaign, this trust is everything.

The Brand's Core: Mission, Vision, and Values

To build a powerful brand, you must first define its core purpose. This is your brand's DNA.

  • Mission (The "What"): This is your purpose. What does your campaign do every day? It should be a clear, concise statement.
    Example for an ocean cleanup campaign: "Our mission is to organize community cleanups to remove plastic from local beaches."
  • Vision (The "Why"): This is the big future you are trying to create. If you succeed, what will the world look like? It should be inspiring.
    Example: "Our vision is a world with plastic-free oceans for future generations."
  • Values (The "How"): These are the principles that guide your actions. How will you behave while working towards your mission? Choose 3-5 words that represent your team's character.
    Example Values: Community, Action, Education, Sustainability.

Branding with Responsibility (Digital Citizenship)

As you design, always think about inclusivity. Your visual identity should represent the diverse community you want to engage. Avoid stereotypes and ensure your brand feels welcoming and respectful to everyone.

Your Digital Campaign Portfolio

Heads up! All the main activities you complete will become part of your professional campaign portfolio. This will be a document or website that showcases your entire process and final work.

Portfolio Structure

Your portfolio should follow this structure to keep it organized and professional. You can use Google Docs, Slides, or even a Google Site to build it.

  • Cover Page
    • Official campaign name and team members' names.
  • Section 1: The Origin of Our Cause (Lesson 2)
    • Your designed Cause Definition Card.
    • Reflection: A paragraph explaining why your team chose this cause and what you hope to achieve.
  • Section 2: The Identity of Our Brand (Lesson 3)
    • The Brand's DNA (Mission, Vision, and Values defined).
    • The final Moodboard.
    • Reflection: A paragraph explaining how the moodboard (colors, images, etc.) represents your brand's DNA.

Main Activity: Create Your Campaign's Moodboard

Now, your team will translate your cause into a visual language by creating a moodboard in Canva. This will be the visual guide for your entire project and the second piece of your portfolio.

  1. Define Your Brand's DNA (5 mins): In your portfolio document, under "Section 2," write down your campaign's Mission, Vision, and at least three core Values. You can use the AI-generated ideas as inspiration.
  2. Build Your Moodboard in Canva (15 mins):
    • Go to Canva.com and create a new design (a "Moodboard Photo Collage" template is a great start).
    • Based on your brand's DNA, search for images, colors, and font styles that capture the feeling of your campaign.
    • For example: If one of your values is "Community," search for photos of diverse people working together. If your cause is environmental, find colors that reflect nature.
    • Arrange these elements on your canvas. Don't worry about making it perfect; focus on collecting ideas that represent your brand's personality.
